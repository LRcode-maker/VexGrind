We coded our robot to be stuck in an infinite loop, trapped inside 4 barriers.

01 Overview

We coded our robot to be stuck in an infinite loop, trapped inside 4 barriers. We placed 4 boxes in a square shape and every time our robot's bumper sensor collided with a box it reversed, turned 90 degrees, and moved forward to the next box. It repeated this loop continuously and the most we tested was around 3 and a half minutes (we have not tested if it can go longer).

🔁 How it works

4 boxes arranged in a square · bumper sensor detects collision · reverse 425 mm · turn right 95° · drive forward again · repeat forever.

02 The Code

The program uses a simple forever loop in VEXcode. The robot drives forward until either BumperA or BumperH is pressed, then reverses 425 mm, turns right 95 degrees, and repeats.

VEXcode V5 Blocks

03 The Outcome

The robot successfully ran the infinite loop for the duration of our testing. The longest continuous run we recorded was approximately 3 and a half minutes — and we have not yet tested how much longer it can sustain the loop.
